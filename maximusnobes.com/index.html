From West African Fields to Bay Area Politics

I believe progress begins with clarity. At its core, strategic communication is the art of simplifying complexity, of finding the central issue within a tangled problem and executing a clear, actionable plan.

I honed this skill in the U.S. Peace Corps in Guinea, where my role was to translate complex agricultural science into practical, relevant training for local farmers. Success wasn't just about what I said, but about building a shared understanding of the 'why' behind a new idea

Today, as a Public Affairs Fellow, I'm learning how to apply that same principle to help our clients navigate the complexities of public opinion and policy, crafting messages that are not just heard, but understood.

Capstone Thesis

The Susu House and Home

My honors capstone thesis, "The Susu House and Home," was an in-depth analysis of how vernacular architecture communicates identity and social ideals within Susu communities in Guinea. The project, grounded in ethnographic fieldwork conducted during my two years of Peace Corps service, explored how practical choices in home-building are powerful acts of communication.

The core argument of my thesis was that these homes function as tangible narratives. I demonstrated that everyday decisions: from the choice of construction materials to the social use of different spaces, are deliberate "projects of self-making" that signal a homeowner's values and their standing within their community.

This project was fundamentally an exercise in strategic analysis, requiring me to:

- Conduct deep qualitative research to understand the "why" behind a community's practical choices.

- Deconstruct how physical objects and spaces tell a story about identity and aspiration.

- Connect hyper-local findings to broader global themes of housing and opportunity.

Ultimately, the thesis shows how to uncover a community's core values by analyzing the practical, everyday choices they make, a skill directly applicable to strategic communications, where understanding an audience's underlying motivations is paramount.

Automating Workflows

Automated Political Insight Generation

I developed a sophisticated workflow that transforms dense, multi-page polling survey PDFs into concise, client-ready one-page summaries. The system uses a two-agent AI process to first identify relevant data and then extract specific data points, automatically populating them into a formatted template. This automation reduces a process that previously took up to an hour of manual work to under five minutes, ensuring speed and accuracy in delivering critical insights to campaign teams and clients.

Automated Voter Dashboard Generator

I created a system that generates a dynamic stakeholder dashboard in Excel for clients. A key feature of this system is an integrated chatbot that allows users to search the PDI (Political Data Inc.) database directly from the dashboard, providing on-demand access to crucial stakeholder information. This transforms a static report into an interactive intelligence tool for making faster, more informed outreach decisions.
